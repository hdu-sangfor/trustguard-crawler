In 2026, the Identity & Access Management (IAM) sector continues to move beyond its traditional role in cybersecurity. The expanding scope and complexity of modern IT environments have significantly increased attack surfaces and introduced new vulnerabilities, particularly in distributed and AI-powered systems.
"IAM has become the primary control layer for enterprise risks, trust, and compliance."
This newsletter provides the latest updates on the key IAM trends defining the landscape in 2026 and their implications for businesses and security professionals.
1. Identity as the Security Perimeter
The traditional network boundaries have long disappeared, and the zero-trust model is the new normal. IAM solutions use identity as the foundation for every access decision, whether it involves humans or machines. It ensures continuous verification based on context, identity, and behavior.
Identity is now the central control plane for access decisions across cloud, SaaS, APIs, and AI systems.
For organizations still operating within legacy IAM systems, this represents significant exposure to password compromise, insider threats, and privilege misuse.
2. IAM Driven by AI Threat Detection
Artificial intelligence is revolutionizing the way IAM works, particularly in identifying security threats and responding to them effectively.
What's changing?
  
  
  
  
AI is increasingly being weaponized through techniques such as prompt injection and deepfake attacks, requiring IAM to evolve as a critical line of defense.
